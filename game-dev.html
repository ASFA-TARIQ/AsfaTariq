Marshmallow Labyrinth

2D Maze Adventure Game Development

Abstract

Marshmallow Labyrinth is a 2D maze-based adventure game that combines exploration, item collection, and combat mechanics within a visually engaging candy-themed environment.

The project demonstrates core game development skills including player movement, enemy behavior, collision systems, shooting mechanics, UI integration, and level design.

Game Logic Flowchart

The gameplay flow of Marshmallow Labyrinth follows a structured progression that guides the player from the main menu to level completion. The player begins by selecting the game from the main menu and enters the labyrinth where the primary objective is to explore the maze and collect all Ginger Bears before reaching the exit.

During gameplay, the player must avoid or shoot enemies while navigating through the maze. Each collision with the enemy reduces the player's health and restarts the level. Only after collecting every Ginger Bear can the player successfully exit the maze and progress to the next level.

As levels increase, the labyrinth becomes larger, enemies become more challenging, and the number of collectibles increases. This flowchart visually represents the logical sequence of the gameplay process including player actions, decision points, enemy interactions, and level progression.

Gameplay Mechanics

The gameplay of Marshmallow Labyrinth is built around exploration, strategy, and interaction with different game elements inside a maze-based environment. Players must navigate through the labyrinth, avoid or defeat enemies, and collect important items in order to progress through each level. The mechanics are designed to create a balance between exploration and challenge while maintaining a clear objective for the player.


Player Movement
The player controls a marshmallow character that moves through the labyrinth using the Arrow Keys or WASD keys. These controls allow the player to navigate different paths in the maze while exploring hidden passages and locating collectibles. Smooth movement and directional animations help the player clearly understand the direction of movement and maintain control while navigating through complex maze layouts.


Enemy System
The game includes enemies represented as vending machines that patrol different areas of the labyrinth. These enemies follow specific movement patterns such as horizontal movement, jumping, or following the player within defined boundaries. If the player collides with an enemy, one health point is lost and the player is reset to the starting position. The player has three health points, and losing all health results in a game over.


Collecting Items
While exploring the maze, the player must collect Ginger Bears and coins scattered throughout the level. Ginger Bears are the primary collectible and must all be gathered before the player can exit the level. This mechanic encourages players to explore the entire maze instead of moving directly toward the exit. Coins add additional rewards and make exploration more engaging.

Level Progression
The game contains four progressively challenging levels. Each level increases in difficulty by introducing larger maze layouts, more collectibles, and more complex enemy movement patterns. Early levels help the player understand the core mechanics, while later levels test the player’s navigation skills, strategy, and ability to manage enemy interactions while collecting all required items.



Design & Development

The design and development of Marshmallow Labyrinth focused on creating a visually engaging environment while maintaining clear gameplay mechanics and player accessibility. The game combines creative visual design with structured level planning to deliver an immersive maze exploration experience. Each visual and gameplay element was carefully designed to support the overall theme and gameplay objectives.

Character / Enemy Design
The main character of the game is a marshmallow, chosen to match the candy-themed environment of the labyrinth. The character is designed to be simple, recognizable, and easy to distinguish from enemies and collectible items. The player character includes directional animations that change based on movement, helping the player clearly understand the character’s orientation while navigating through the maze. In contrast, the enemy characters are represented as vending machines that patrol different areas of the labyrinth. These enemies follow specific movement patterns such as horizontal movement, jumping, or tracking the player within defined boundaries. Their unpredictable movement adds challenge to the gameplay and requires players to carefully navigate the maze while avoiding or attacking the enemy.

Maze Design
The labyrinth environment is inspired by a Candy Land theme, where the walls and surroundings include various sweet-themed elements such as cakes, gingerbread houses, moon cakes, baklava, and candies. The ground uses a wafer-like pattern to further reinforce the candy environment. These visual elements make the maze more engaging and visually appealing while still maintaining clear pathways for navigation.

Level Design
The game consists of four levels, each designed with increasing complexity and difficulty. Early levels introduce the player to the core mechanics of movement, exploration, and item collection. As the player progresses, the maze becomes larger and more complex, enemies become more challenging, and the number of collectibles increases. This gradual increase in difficulty ensures that players can learn the game mechanics while still being challenged in later stages.

Audio System
Audio plays an important role in enhancing the gameplay experience. The game includes background music as well as sound effects for actions such as collecting coins, collecting Ginger Bears, winning a level, and losing the game. An audio manager system was implemented to control the playback of music and sound effects, allowing the player to adjust the volume according to their preferences through the settings menu.



Key Features and Functional Highlights

The technical implementation of Marshmallow Labyrinth focuses on building interactive gameplay systems using scripting to control player actions, enemy behavior, item collection, and audio management.


Enemy Controller Script

The enemy controller manages vending machine enemies that patrol the maze. A mathematical oscillation function allows the enemy to move continuously between boundaries, creating unpredictable movement patterns. 


          void Update()
          {
              movement.y = Mathf.PingPong(Time.time * EnemySpeed, 8) - 4;
              movement.x = 0;
          }

Reflection & Future Improvements

Developing Marshmallow Labyrinth was a valuable learning experience that allowed me to explore different aspects of game design and programming. Through this project, I gained practical experience in implementing gameplay mechanics, designing user interfaces, and debugging issues that occurred during development. Working through the process of testing and refining the game helped improve my problem-solving skills and understanding of how different systems interact within a game environment.

One of the most rewarding aspects of the project was combining multiple gameplay elements such as maze exploration, item collection, enemy interaction, and player combat into a single cohesive experience. The testing phase also provided important insights into player behavior and game difficulty, which helped improve the gameplay balance.

In the future, several improvements could enhance the game further. Additional enemy types with different behaviors could be introduced to create more dynamic challenges. More levels could be added with increasingly complex maze layouts and obstacles. The combat system could also be improved by introducing enemy attacks or more advanced player abilities. These enhancements would expand the gameplay experience and provide players with greater variety and challenge.
